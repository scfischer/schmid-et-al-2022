Detailed Description

This class handles oxygen uptake and oxygen-saturation-related tinting of erythrocytes as they pass through the capillary.

Erythrocytes are the main agents for transporting oxygen in the blood stream. Along their passage through the alveolar capillaries - represented in this model by their their traversal of a single, representative capillary - oxygen diffuses from the blood plasma into the erythrocytes where it binds to haemoglobin.

The changing oxygen saturation of haemoglobin is visually represented by change of tint of the erythrocyte. The exact tint results from an interpolation between the oxygenated and deoxygenated tint colours, blended with the model material's initial tint colour.
